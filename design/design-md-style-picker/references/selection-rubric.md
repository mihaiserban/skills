# Selection Rubric

Use this when a product brief could fit multiple DESIGN.md profiles.

## Product-Type Matches

- Developer infrastructure: Vercel, Supabase, ClickHouse, Stripe, HashiCorp, Replicate, Resend, Warp.
- AI and model products: Claude, Cohere, RunwayML, Mistral AI, ElevenLabs, Together AI, Minimax, x.ai.
- Dense productivity tools: Linear, Superhuman, Raycast, Notion, Airtable, Cal.com.
- Creative or visual tools: Figma, Framer, Webflow, Miro, Clay, Pinterest, RunwayML.
- Fintech and trading: Stripe, Wise, Coinbase, Kraken, Binance, Revolut, Mastercard.
- Retail and consumer: Apple, Nike, Airbnb, Shopify, Starbucks, Spotify, Meta.
- Editorial and media: WIRED, The Verge, RunwayML, Pinterest.
- Automotive and premium object pages: Ferrari, Bugatti, Lamborghini, BMW, BMW M, Tesla, Renault.
- Retro or nostalgic web: Dell 1996, Nintendo 2001.

## Decision Heuristics

- If the product is a working tool, prefer workflow density over spectacle.
- If the product sells a physical object, prefer image strategy and spatial drama.
- If the product sells trust, prefer disciplined palette, stable typography, and conservative motion.
- If the product sells creativity, prefer media systems, expressive layout, and one memorable interaction.
- If the user asks for "premium," decide whether premium means sparse luxury, technical precision, editorial confidence, or consumer polish before choosing.

## Red Flags

- A style chosen only because the accent color sounds nice.
- A marketing-first source chosen for a data-heavy workspace.
- A famous brand style chosen when the product needs a quieter category convention.
- Two or more styles mixed before one clear source has been explored deeply.
